# Region 01 — Reedwater Basin

**Status:** Vertical slice region  
**Hub settlement:** Lilypond  
**Dominant peoples:** Frog, toad, turtle folk

## Overview

The Reedwater Basin is a wide, slow river valley where the Meridian's tributary spreads into oxbow lakes and reed marshes. For centuries it has been a trade corridor between upland stone quarries and estuary salt markets. Life here is measured in water levels.

## Lilypond (Major Settlement)

**Population:** ~400 (mixed amphibian majority)  
**Government:** Council of Shells — elected elders from each species quarter  
**Economy:** River trade, reed weaving, fish curing, turtle masonry exports

### Districts

| District | Species lean | Character |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Lilymarket | Frog merchants | Floating stalls, quick deals, leap-ways between platforms |
| Mudwall | Turtle masons | Stone flood walls, slow craftsmanship, highest buildings |
| Croakend | Toad artisans | Pottery, toxins-as-medicine, night markets |
| Reedwalk | Mixed housing | Stilt homes, family ponds |

### Architecture

- **Frogs:** Light stilt platforms, rope bridges, open-air stalls
- **Turtles:** Domed shell-inspired stone, low and wide, flood-resistant
- **Toads:** Burrow-shops with clay facades, sunken courtyards for humidity

## Minor Sites

### Sunken Chapel (Ruin)

Pre-council temple half-submerged after the **Great Spill** (see HISTORY.md). Environmental storytelling: faded murals of three species kneeling at one river. Investigation site for vertical slice quest.

### Ferryman's Rest (Outpost)

Single-family toad ferry operation. Neutral ground. Gossip hub. Player hears rumor hooks here.

## Friction Point — The Flood Levy Dispute

**Cause:** Turtle masons built a new levy north of Lilypond to protect Mudwall. Downstream frog merchants say it channels floodwater into Lilymarket.  
**Who benefits:** Mudwall residents, upland stone trade (dry roads).  
**Who suffers:** Lilymarket traders, ferry operators (erratic currents).  
**Player stakes:** Council vote approaches at end of flood season. Choices shift prices, dialogue, and NPC schedules.
