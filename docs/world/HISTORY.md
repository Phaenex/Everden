# Reedwater Basin History

History explains the present. Players uncover it through ruins, dialogue, and investigation.

## Founding Myth (Shared)

Before Lilypond, three peoples argued over a single pond. The frogs wanted open water for trade. The toads wanted shaded banks for burrows. The turtles wanted raised stone to survive floods. None would yield.

According to **The First Ripple** and **The Long Shell** traditions alike, a stranger (interpreted differently by each species) showed them an oxbow lake — wide water, muddy banks, and a natural berm. They built together. The stranger left no name. Frog songs call them the **Skipped Stone**. Turtles call them the **First Architect**. Toads say the earth still remembers their footsteps.

## The Great Spill (87 years ago)

A turtle levy upstream failed during record rains. Lilypond's old temple district flooded overnight. The **Sunken Chapel** remains half-underwater. 200 died. Trust between upstream masons and downstream merchants cracked.

**Present effect:** Frog folk distrust new levies. Turtle folk insist flood walls are non-negotiable.

## Historical Victory — The Charter of Shells (52 years ago)

After a brief trade war (boats blockaded, burrows flooded intentionally), three species signed the **Charter of Shells**. Council governance, shared flood defense fund, neutral ferry rights for toads.

**Present effect:** Legal framework for current levy dispute. Council vote is the peaceful path.

## Great Failure — The Blackfen Drainage (21 years ago)

A frog-led project drained part of Blackfen for farmland. Summer fish kills, bog fire, salamander enclave displaced (they left; not in slice). Project abandoned.

**Present effect:** Environmental skepticism. "Quick fixes" are politically toxic.

## Ancient Enemy (Dormant)

**The Dried Crown** — legend of a pre-amphibian kingdom that tried to dam the entire Meridian. Ruins upstream; not in slice. Foreshadowing only.

## Forgotten Civilization

Murals in Sunken Chapel depict a fourth species silhouette — possibly salamander folk. Erased from charter records. Investigation hook.

## Legends

- **The Walking Levy** — turtle ghost said to patrol flooded causeways at night
- **Pip's Luck** — frog traders swear by leaping at dawn on market days
