# Everden Geography

Geography comes first. Civilization follows terrain.

## Continental Overview

Everden's known world spans a single supercontinent, **Althara**, broken by inland seas and a spine of highlands running north-south. Player-facing content begins in the **Low Meridian** — a humid belt of river basins, wetlands, and temperate forest margins.

## Biomes

| Biome | Climate | Key resources | Travel notes |
|-------|---------|---------------|--------------|
| River basin | Humid temperate | Fish, clay, reeds, timber | Boats fastest; mud slows foot |
| Wetland margin | Seasonal flood | Lily tubers, peat, herbs | Impassable in deep flood |
| Upland forest | Cooler, drier | Stone, oak, game | Roads reliable year-round |
| Coastal estuary | Salt + fresh mix | Salt, shell, trade | Tidal gates affect ferries |

## Water Systems

The **Meridian River** drains the Low Meridian into the Grey Estuary. Tributaries include:

- **Reedwater** — slow, wide, oxbow lakes (slice hub)
- **Stonecut Creek** — fast, rocky, upland source
- **Blackfen Channel** — peat bog outlet, navigable only in dry season

## Resource Distribution

- **Clay + reeds:** abundant along Reedwater banks → frog/toad pottery and weaving
- **Stone:** upland quarries → turtle masonry, flood walls
- **Fish:** year-round staple; salmon run in autumn
- **Lily tubers:** wetland harvest, flood-dependent pricing

## Weather Patterns

| Season | Basin weather | Gameplay effect |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Spring | Rains, rising water | Mud terrain, amphibian advantage |
| Summer | Warm, stable | Peak trade, lowest fish prices |
| Autumn | Fog, salmon run | Reduced visibility, fish glut |
| Winter | Frost, low water | Frozen shallows, higher firewood demand |

## Seasonal Effects

- **Flood season (late spring):** southern causeway submerged; ferry prices double
- **Dry season (late summer):** Blackfen Channel closes to boats
- **First frost:** amphibian folk move slower at dawn unless near heat source
