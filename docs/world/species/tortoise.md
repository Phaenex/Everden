# Tortoise Folk

**Combat role:** Tank (chronicle-minded)  
**Homeland affinity:** Stone foundations, river bends, flood walls — same basin as Turtle Folk, longer memory

## Biology

- Heavier shell than river turtles; slower on land, nearly immovable when braced
- Can withdraw for long periods; metabolism even slower than turtle kin
- Live 120–200 years; elders remember floods by name
- Herbivorous; jaw strength for grinding reed roots and shell-hardening minerals

## Culture

### Build
Same domed masonry tradition as Turtle Folk, with more emphasis on **archive vaults** and flood-mark chronicles carved into lintels.

### Fight
Shield walls and choke points like turtles, but tortoise units prefer **holding ground for allies** over advancing — "the line remembers."

### Hunt / Farm
Raised berms, slow orchards, algae pens. Less boat travel than turtles; more overland relay paths between archive towns.

### Worship
**The Long Shell** — shared with Turtle Folk, with tortoise temples keeping the **year-rings**: stone cylinders recording each flood season.

### Travel
Plodding caravans between archive towns; rarely the first to arrive, always the last to forget.

### Government
Council of Shells plurality held by turtle and tortoise elders; frogs and toads hold charter minority seats.

### Family
Few offspring; shell-hall communal raising. Tortoise elders often serve as **basin historians** alongside masons.

### Death
Shells become foundation stones or archive markers. "Returned to the wall, returned to the record."
